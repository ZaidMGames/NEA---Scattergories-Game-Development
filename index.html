Scattergories Rules

Scattergories is a fun and fast-paced game that challenges players to come up with unique and creative answers to a variety of categories. Here are the rules of how to play Scattergories:

Objective:

The objective of the game is to score the most points by coming up with unique answers to various categories. Do you Think you are smart enough?

Gameplay:

  1. To start the game, press the start button obviously. Players can choose to see what the chosen letter is prior to starting the round by pressing the chosen letter button. If you want to keep the chosen letter, a mystery, don't press the chosen Letter button and just start the round
  2. Once the letter is chosen, a list of categories (default number of categories is set to 12) will be revealed on the game board.
  3. The timer will begin (default timer value is 60s), and players will have that time to come up with an answer for each category that begins with the chosen letter.
  4. Each answer must be unique and cannot be repeated by another player. You are not allowed to use the same word more than once per round.
  5. Once the timer runs out, players will take turns sharing their answers for each category. If another player has the same answer, both players will not score points
  6. Players will score points for each unique answer that has also been validated by the other players. [other scoring rules will be specified below]
  7. The player with the most points at the end of the round is declared the winner.

Voting/Scoring:

For the most part, what counts as a valid answer is subjective and depends on the people you are playing with.

The word The does not count for the letter T. — For example, The Matrix is valid for Movies/ M but not Movies / T>. — The same is true for the words A and An

You can begin with a person's last name.

— For example, Depp, Johnny is valid for Celebrities / D .

Avoid descriptive adjectives unless they are part of a common phrase.

— Ask yourself: is the word that follows the adjective doing most of the heavy lifting? — For example, Vegetables / G: Green Beans vs Green Lettuce.

  • Players will earn one point for each unique answer.
  • If a player is the only one to have an answer for a category, they will earn two points.

End of the Game:

The game can be played in multiple rounds, with each round consisting of a different letter. The player with the most points at the end of all rounds is declared the overall winner.

Additional Notes:

  • All answers must be within the bounds of good taste and common sense.
  • Players cannot use proper nouns, such as names of people, places, or brands.
  • If a player cannot think of an answer for a category, they can skip that category and move on to the next one.
  • Any disputes about whether an answer is valid should be settled by a vote among the players.
